### Discussion questions

Reflect on these questions and bring your notes to the live session:

1. **What makes a "good" solution?** Think beyond technical correctnessâ€”what other criteria matter for solutions that actually get implemented and make a difference?

2. **How should consultants balance evidence with other considerations?** When is evidence paramount, and when might other factors (values, politics, practical constraints) legitimately take precedence?

3. **What are the key ethical challenges at this stage?** How do ethical considerations differ when designing and presenting solutions compared to the diagnostic phase?

4. **How can consultants build genuine ownership of solutions?** What specific tactics help move from "consultant's recommendation" to "client's solution"?

5. **How do you handle resistance to recommendations?** What strategies work for different types of resistance?

### Live session focus

The live session will be discussion-based rather than additional lecture. We'll explore:

- **Solution design**: How to develop recommendations that are technically sound, evidence-informed, and practically implementable
- **Presenting recommendations**: Structuring feedback for action rather than just information transfer
- **Ethical navigation**: Working through genuine dilemmas rather than easy cases
- **The evidence debate**: When and how to use evidence-based approaches

- How would you present your recommendations to gain acceptance?
- What ethical considerations are relevant to your proposed solutions?
